Enron

The company that changed trust into betrayal.

Enron's logo, at the bottom floor of the Enron's building before everything had collapsed.

Here's a time line of Enron:

  • 1985 - Enron formed through the merger of Houston Natural Gas and InterNorth, led by Kenneth Lay as CEO, focusing on natural gas pipelines.
  • 1987-1990 - Expands into energy trading and international markets; Jeffrey Skilling joins, pushing for mark-to-market accounting.
  • 1990s - Becomes deregulated energy market leader, diversifying into electricity, broadband, and global projects like Dabhol in India.
  • 1996-2000 - Peak growth; stock price soars (hits $90/share in 2000). Skilling becomes COO, then CEO in 2001. Aggressive accounting hides debt via Special Purpose Entities (SPEs) like those managed by Andy Fastow.
  • 2001 -
  • Feb: Skilling named CEO; Lay remains chairman.
  • Aug: Skilling resigns abruptly; Lay resumes CEO role. Whistleblower Sherron Watkins warns of accounting issues.
  • Oct: Enron reports $638M Q3 loss, $1.2B shareholder equity write-down. SEC launches investigation.
  • Nov: Admits inflating earnings by $586M since 1997. Stock plummets below $1.
  • Dec: Files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy (largest in U.S. history at the time, $63.4B in assets).
  • 2002: - Arthur Andersen, Enron’s auditor, collapses due to shredding documents. Criminal investigations intensify.
  • 2004-2006 -
  • Lay and Skilling found guilty of fraud and conspiracy (2006). Lay dies before sentencing; Skilling sentenced to 24 years (later reduced).
  • Fastow pleads guilty, sentenced to 6 years.
  • 2008 - Enron's remnants sold off; company effectively ceases to exist.
  • Legacy - Leads to Sarbanes-Oxley Act (2002) for corporate accountability. Enron becomes a symbol of corporate fraud.

"Enron's dazzling ascent, fueled by our unmatched brilliance and boundless innovation, proves we're the gold standard of corporate success—trust us, our books are flawless!"

— Enron, circa 2000, before its spectacular implosion revealed a house of cards built on creative accounting and hubris.
